# Summary

## **Chapter 3: Sal Has Written a Play**

The guests of the Bed & Breakfast decide to stage a performance of Sal's work-in-progress play; a love triangle, he explains, between Isabello the captain of the guard, Captain Redtooth the pirate, and Roberto the banished wizard. Gertrude, Amélie, and one of the rabbits take the leading roles; Percy is cast as Bumblelina the Fairy Queen; and Bud is chosen to play Newt, a character oddly reminiscent of the playwright himself. The remaining rabbits are assigned a dragon and a tree.

The production is a disaster. The rabbits treat rehearsal like playtime, ignoring their scripts in favor of hurling imaginary fire and thunder at each other. Amélie struggles to connect with the romantic themes; Bud is seized by stage fright. Gertrude gives a great performance, but it isn't enough. While the scenes of love and sorrow seem to stir something in Percy, having him sob through the whole show, Sal still decides to shut the production down mid-performance, visibly upset.

## **Chapter 4: The Breakfast Feast**

With the mood hanging low, Hey Kid suggests surprising the Bed & Breakfast with a big breakfast feast. Parish warms to the idea, and soon Yola and the Ghoul Gang are rolling up their sleeves too.

The group dives into assembling a proper brunch: deviled eggs, biscuits, ramelettes (ramen omelets), and Parish's famous rollmops. The frog quickly learns that sharing a kitchen is its own challenge, but things start to progress after he ships Hey Kid off to toast duty, though they lose interest almost immediately. Then, even the Ghoul Gang join in the biscuit-making with Yola.

Keeping the young ones focused proves nearly impossible. Every new dish somehow ends in a food fight, leaving the kitchen trashed each time. Parish eventually snaps and launches the biggest food fight of all which, naturally, sends everything they'd worked on straight to the walls, floor, and ceiling.

But Parish has one last trick: buried in the back, he unearths a stash of cereal boxes from his tadpole days: discontinued classics he'd been hoarding for years. Mucky Charms, Crusted Flakes, Foot Loops, Cocoa Muffs, Lice Crispies… A homemade brunch would have been something, but an exotic cereal buffet? Not too shabby at all.

# Summary

## **Chapter 1: Another Rainy Day**

It's raining outside, and the guests of Yazeba's Bed & Breakfast are doing their best to pass the time. The rabbits, usually outside in the garden, have decided to stay in and attempt to play games as to not ruin their little bunny outfits. Hey Kid, however, has little luck killing time, and all that cooped up energy seems to build up inside of them: any minute now, they are literally about to explode.

Gertrude, wanting to avoid Hey Kid's boredom destroying the B&B, invites the bunnies to play with the two of them. Rag-and-Bones, passing by, notices the chaos might be the perfect distraction to steal Yazeba's spellbook, so he puts on suspenders and a helicopter hat and pretends to be a child named "Um, Jim" to join in.

They try a crab-walk race, then double-dog dares, then a pillow fight that ends with Hey Kid turning into a fireball and blowing the bunnies up to the ceiling fan. Then Rag-and-Bones proposes his own game, "Apocalypse Now": sneak into Yazeba's room while she naps and steal an object. Whoever brings back her spell book wins.

The bunnies go first. After making quite some noise, they return with the winning item and hand it to the unpleasant skeleton man. He opens it, disappointed: it's a dictionary. This book will help him spell, the bunnies say.

As the afternoon winds down, everyone decides to slide down the stairs in boxes, which ends in disaster. Rag-and-Bones kicks the bunnies' box through the window and into the yard, and Hey Kid crashes into Gertrude, which turns out to be exactly what they needed to finally calm down. As they start smelling what Parish is making for dinner, even Rag-and-Bones must admit he had fun today.

## **Chapter 2: The Witch's Missing Shadow**

Yazeba's shadow has slipped away again, and it's wrecking the Bed & Breakfast, to Amelie's dismay. Fortunately, Parish, finishing up the dishes, hears the ruckus and joins in to help. Less fortunately, Monday, the fairy dream merchant, also decides to tag along.

Catching the shadow is tougher than it looks. Amelie is too busy cleaning up after it, Parish keeps charging headfirst with his frying pan and wrecking things even more, and Monday mostly uses the situation to leverage a deal. They strike a deal with Parish: the fairy will help catching the shadow in exchange for a three-course egg breakfast tomorrow morning.

The hunt leads them to Lady Sheridan's private bathroom, where Parish attempts a duel and ends up wrapped in a shower curtain and dunked in the toilet. He must admit his knighting days might be quite behind him.

After being called useless one too many times, Monday finally shows off, conjuring a sparkling of fairy dust that traps the shadow in light and lets Yazeba capture it in a jar. "Wasn't it nice to be helpful?" the frog asks, but Monday shrugs it off. The whole thing was just getting annoying.

Parish promises Monday their three eggs tomorrow. Monday corrects him: three _courses_. But Parish argues that for a frog, an egg is a course. They should have read the fine print.

Finally, Yazeba hands the jar to Amelie, who carries it to the basement and places it among a wall of fifty jars just like it, each one holding a restless, spiteful shadow trying to escape.

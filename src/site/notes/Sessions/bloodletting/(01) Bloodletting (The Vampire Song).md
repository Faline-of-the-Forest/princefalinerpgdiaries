# Summary

## **Chapter 1: I'm the Walking Dead**

Saturday, January 6th, 1990. Twelfth Night marks the start of Carnival season in New Orleans, but the streets and clubs of the French Quarter are no busier than usual. Conqueror Worm, the newest cult sludge metal band out of the Big Easy, is playing at Tipitina's, and up in the rafters, a cloaked Nosferatu watches, hidden.

Years ago, when he was still handsome and lively, it was at this very venue that he met his future fiancée. Tonight, she goes by a different name, Eleanor, and she's the front singer of Conqueror Worm. Elvys meant to finally speak to her, to try and get her to remember him, but he was about to lose his nerve when he saw Joey, the guitarist, start making out with her. It drove him to frenzied anger. He dropped from the ceiling and attacked Joey.

In his frenzy, he also attacked Eleanor, choking her before getting his ass kicked by Joey. Elvys fled, only to bump into Donovan, the Sheriff of New Orleans, on his way out. Donovan gave chase.

As a neonate Embraced without Prince Vidal's permission, Elvys had spent the last two years hidden, dreading the punishment that awaited him. He wasn't fast enough. Unable to squirm free, he was manacled and brought before the Prince.

## **Chapter 2: A Crack in the Mirror**

In the Fellowship Hall of St. John the Baptist Catholic Church, New Orleans' coalition of nonprofits and charities is celebrating Twelfth Night at a humble gathering hosted by Lionel Desdunes, mayoral candidate.

Reggie Corrigan is here only to accompany his colleague and close friend Wendy Arceneaux, but after realizing Desdunes has been staring at him most of the night, he approaches. Soon, Antoine Savoy, the Vampire Lord of the French Quarter, joins the party, seemingly only to discuss business with Reggie. Savoy expresses interest in funding Reggie's operations, though he asks a favor in return: attend a dinner next weekend and endorse Desdunes before next month's election. Reggie understands what's happening: Savoy wants to remove the current mayor, a pawn of Vidal's, and install his own through Desdunes. Reggie considers going, even though he has so far stayed out of politics to avoid drawing attention to himself.

On their way home, Reggie and Wendy come across a frenzied vampire in the middle of a meal. Reggie stays calm long enough to yell at Wendy to run, but he is soon overcome by fear and flees as well, leaving the frenzied Beast behind.

## **Chapter 3: A Rocking Chair by the Window**

Amélie is in the middle of her shift at her own shop, the Telfort Herbarium, when Caroline, her teenaged guest, comes home with a bloody nose and a handful of bruises. Amélie repeats, once more, that she doesn't think it's a good idea for the young Lupine to chauffeur for the biggest criminal organization in New Orleans, reminding her that if she were to transform, Vidal would have her hunted down and killed. Caroline seems to think that since there hasn't been an "incident" in months, she's fully in control.

Soon after, Amélie gets another visitor: Lidia Kendall, close companion to Baron Cimitiere, who has come to ask a favor. Amélie understands that the Baron is researching the city's ley lines, and they need a map that would have been created in the 19th century. Out of places to look, and with the New Orleans chapter of the Ordo Dracul too recently established to have records that old, they're turning to Amélie, whose grandmother kept copious notes on the occult side of the Big Sleep. Amélie finds what Lidia is looking for, but will only share it if she's roped into whatever the Baron is working on. Lidia agrees to her terms and promises to bring her to the Baron the following night to look at it together.

After Lidia's departure, Amélie hears a commotion in the street: cops are ganging up on Malia Eliza Curry, Cimitiere's protégée, who strangely does not seem able to fight back. Amélie uses her Cruàc powers to rouse the Beast within Malia, causing her to frenzy and kill the attacking officers. She then tries to use the same powers to cripple the frenzied vampire in place, but the Beast resists and slips into the night.

## **Chapter 4: The Ways and Means**

Andy Deveraux is invited to the Perdido House to meet with the Prince, who has asked for his counsel. Vidal explains his predicament: Caitlin Meadows, his former hound, disappeared months ago and has since resurfaced, gone rogue. Since her return, she has destroyed five vampires and attempted to destroy his rival, Antoine Savoy. Before the Kindred of New Orleans come to believe Vidal is sending his hound to attack rivals like a brute, she needs to be dealt with. But calling a blood hunt on his own hound would make him look weak, so he asks Andy to handle the matter quietly. Once Meadows is destroyed, Vidal will pardon him. He also mentions that with Meadows gone wrong, the position of Hound is open, and if Andy does a good job, he might have an opportunity. Andy decides to take the job.

## **Chapter 5: A Bloodstain on the Bed**

After making it home safely, Reggie spent the rest of the night cooling down, until he saw, from his window, a shadow slipping through Wendy's window across the street. He ran across, scaled the same wall the shadow had climbed, and pulled himself through into her bedroom. Inside, a grisly scene.

The bed drenched in blood, Wendy pale atop it, and the vampire from earlier, perched over her, its mouth on her neck. Reggie tore it off and pummeled it until it stopped moving, then pulled its head from its shoulders. He turned back to Wendy, called an ambulance, and tried to stabilize her. Yet, as time ran on, he realized she wouldn't make it.

Feeling no other choice, and knowing that creating a childe without Prince Vidal's permission was a crime worthy of death in his domain, Reggie still chose to save Wendy by Embracing her.

When her pale lips closed around the bleeding wound on his hand to drink his Vitae, he knew he had Damned her.

As he heard the ambulance approach, he took Wendy in his arms and carried her to his own haven, where he could watch over his childe.

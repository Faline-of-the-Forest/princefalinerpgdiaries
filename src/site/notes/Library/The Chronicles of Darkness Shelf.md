## Chronicles of Darkness

### **The World of Darkness** (2004)
![book_cod_chroniclesofdarkness.png\|left hsmall](/img/user/Images/Books/book_cod_chroniclesofdarkness.png)
**Author:** Bill Bridges, Rick Chillot, Ken Cliffe and Mike Lee
**Pages**: 224
**Description**: A **system** set in a modern gothic world, here inviting players to take the roles of ordinary people on the edge of the supernatural and its dark, twisted horrors and conspiracies.

<div style="clear:both"></div>

>**Review**:
A thorough—and perhaps too thorough—rulebook that serves as the basis of all monstrous systems released afterwards, this set of rules does a surprisingly great job at being a standalone game, and doesn't run short of ideas to give the Storyteller that will pick this up, as long as they don't feel too intimidated by how dense and unwieldy the game's presentation is.. 
>★★★½

### **Vampire: The Requiem** (2004)

![book_cod_vampiretherequiem.png\|left hsmall](/img/user/Images/Books/book_cod_vampiretherequiem.png)
**Author:** Ari Marmell, Dean Shomshak, C. A. Suleiman
Additional Material: Justin Achilli, Carl Bowen, John Chambers, Matthew McFarland, Sarah Roark, Matthew Rourke and Greg Stolze
**Pages**: 304
**Description**: A **supplement** that lets the players take the role of the Kindred: vampires stuck in an everlasting world of political horror and forced to keep the beast of their hunger leashed, lest they become it.

<div style="clear:both"></div>

>**Review**:
Despite the setting being interesting at times, the writing of the book is victim to too much repetition, which coupled with the dense prose on every page, makes it hard to actually parse through. Despite containing a neat, although outdated, system, the material is tough to chew through, and yet it still manages to present a world of modern gothic horror worth playing in.
>★★★

### **City of the Damned: New Orleans** (2005)

![book_cod_neworleans.png\|left hsmall](/img/user/Images/Books/book_cod_neworleans.png)
**Author:** Ari Marmell and C. A. Suleiman
**Pages**: 144
**Description**: A **setting** describing the current state of Kindred politics in New Orleans, the agendas of the Elders, and the schemes and conspiracies newly arrived neonates might be caught into if they happened to step into the Crescent City.

<div style="clear:both"></div>

>**Review**:
It is disappointing that what is presented as a whole setting for a vampire chronicle turned out to be a short section of dry, uninspiring descriptions of New Orleans' locations, undeveloped story ideas that are at best curious, and mostly a collection of backstories from an abundance of NPCs that could never fit entirely in one campaign. With little helpful material for a Storyteller looking to pay homage to the atmosphere of New Orleans, and too many ideas left for the Storyteller to figure out on their own, one could likely tell just as good as story, if not a better one, by simply sticking to the short New Orleans Appendix from the core book.
>★★½

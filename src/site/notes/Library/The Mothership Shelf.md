# Official Material

### **Dead Planet** (2018)
![book_mothership-deadplanet.png\|left hsmall](/img/user/Images/Books/book_mothership-deadplanet.png)
**Author:**  Donn Stroud, Fiona Maeve & Sean McCoy
**Pages**: 52
**Description**: A **module** in three acts: a "dungeon-crawl" aboard derelict ships, a dive into a cannibal colony on a barren moon, and an exploration of a dead planet that harbors a gate to a dangerous dimension.
<br>
<br>
>**Review**:
While none of the premises hooked me particularly, the sum of its parts is greater than its whole. I could easily imagine developing multiple sessions around any of them, not to mention that it contains very useful tables for weapons cache, nightmares and derelict ships, each useable on their own for engaging results.
>★★★½

### **Gradient Descent** (2021)
![book_mothership-gradientdescent.png\|left hsmall](/img/user/Images/Books/book_mothership-gradientdescent.png)
**Author:** Luke Gearing
**Pages**: 64
**Description**: A **module** set in a derelict android factory, now controlled by Monarch, a rogue artificial intelligence. Known as the Deep, this place draws Divers, who seek valuable artifacts that can be sold for millions, although finding tħem may cost them their sanity.
<br>

>**Review**:
An incredibly engaging sci-fi horror megadungeon packed into just 60 pages. Monarch presents a thrilling antagonist to both play and face off against, and the book is brimming with opportunities to explore and interact with the massive facility. However, while its minimalist design makes it lighter to use at the table, it places significant pressure on the moderator to detail the sprawling factory rooms, sometimes with only a line or two of flavor to go off of.
>★★★★

# Third-Party Material
### **HUNDREDS** (2024)
![book_mothership-hundreds.png\|left hsmall](/img/user/Images/Books/book_mothership-hundreds.png)
**Author:** Quadra
**Pages**: 60
**Description**: A collection of **tables** for generating all sorts of elements, from character relationships, weapons and strange objects, and details on space ports and what happens in them.
<br>
<br>
>**Review**:
One of the most useful resource I have come across for any TTRPG: each table is rich, each containing a hundred interesting prompts. Not all tables are equally handy or exciting, but the bulk and quality of the material here is impressive.
>★★★★½

### **Apothecaria** (2021)
![book_apothecaria.png\|left hsmall](/img/user/Images/Books/book_apothecaria.png)
**Author:** Anna Blackwell
**Pages**: 60
**Description**: A solo **journaling game** where you craft potions in order to support a small, tight-knit community. Using a deck of cards, you'll gather ingredients, cure your neighbors' ailments, and uncover the hidden secrets of the surrounding wilderness.

<div style="clear:both"></div>

>**Review**:
>It has just the right amount of slice-of-life charm for a simple journaling game, but the upgrades and potion equipment management fail to make an interesting system, and lacks the depth to elevate it into something beyond just a decent journaling game.
>★★★

### **Yazeba's Bed & Breakfast** (2024)
![book_yazeba.png\|left hsmall](/img/user/Images/Books/book_yazeba.png)
**Author:** Possum Creek Games
**Pages**: 504
**Description**: A slice-of-life **roleplaying game** about the residents and guests of Yazeba's Bed & Breakfast, as they spend time together on lazy afternoons, frightful nights, and getting up to all sorts of zany antics.

<div style="clear:both"></div>

>**Review**:
>Yazeba manages to bring back the childhood game of pretend, of playing with dolls and action figures, and packages it in a way that players of all ages can get excited over it again. With beautiful art, fun characters, and perfectly crafted slice-of-life chapters, this is a massive labor of love that takes only 15 minutes to get ready for without anyone having to prepare anything in advance: that is the book's real magic trick.
>★★★★★
